Time Value of Money

Power Function · Future Value · Present Value

What is a power?  bⁿ = b × b × b … (n times)
b = base  ·  n = exponent  ·  read as "b raised to the power n" or "b to the n"

The idea

A power is just repeated multiplication. Instead of writing the same number multiplied by itself over and over, we use a small raised number — the exponent — to say how many times.

2¹2  (just 2, once)
2²2 × 2 = 4
2³2 × 2 × 2 = 8
2⁴2 × 2 × 2 × 2 = 16
2⁵2 × 2 × 2 × 2 × 2 = 32
2¹⁰= 1,024

Why decimals work too

The base doesn't have to be a whole number. In the TVM formula the base is (1 + r), which is always a decimal slightly above 1 — for example 1.06 at a 6% rate.

(1.06)¹= 1.06
(1.06)²1.06 × 1.06 = 1.1236
(1.06)³1.06 × 1.06 × 1.06 ≈ 1.1910
(1.06)¹⁰≈ 1.7908
(1.06)²⁰≈ 3.2071
(1.06)³⁰≈ 5.7435
Notice how fast (1.06)ⁿ grows. At 6% interest, money roughly doubles every 12 years (the "Rule of 72": 72 ÷ 6 = 12). Powers are what drives compound growth — each year you earn interest on your interest.

Key rules you'll use

b⁰ = 1Anything to the power 0 is 1
b¹ = bPower of 1 leaves the number unchanged
bᵐ × bⁿ = bᵐ⁺ⁿSame base? Add the exponents
bᵐ ÷ bⁿ = bᵐ⁻ⁿSame base? Subtract the exponents
On your calculator: the button is usually labelled yˣ, xʸ, or ^. To compute (1.06)¹⁰ type 1.06 then yˣ then 10 then =. In Excel or Google Sheets use =1.06^10.
